Land is part of an association with dues. Something like $10/year. You won't even know they exist until the bill comes every year, but they do some good things behind the scenes like road maintenence and ensuring the community well is safe for drinking.

The community well is 3 miles away, and it's $100/yr for a key. Most residents up there keep a water tank on the back of a truck to get water. The cabin/property is plumbed, but there is no large water tank there for holding. There are 2 or 3 50 gallon barrels that we used.

You can drill your own well.

The winters are very harsh and very beautiful. It's a winter wonderland... seriously. But it's also harsh. You should have a way to get to the road in the winter as the roads are not plowed unless you (or other residents) do it yourself. There is a resident who normally plows the first mile down Kodiak from the road... so you will be responsible for the last 1/2 mile. You can pay him or another resident to do it... or you can have a truck with a snow blade to do it yourself. The property comes with a snow blower for your use. That's how we kept the roads open. It works, but it's a lot of work. Some residents use a snowmobile to get to and from a vehicle they keep near the highway.

This is wilderness. Pure and beautiful and full of life.
