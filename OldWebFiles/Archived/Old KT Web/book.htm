Answer violence with Peace

The Old Testament book of Leviticus promotes “eye for eye, tooth for tooth” as the answer to conflict; but Yeshua has a different way to handle it.  A “New Law of Retaliation,” as it were: 

“’You have heard the commandment, ‘An eye for an eye, a tooth for a tooth.’  But what I say to you is:  offer no resistance to injury.  When a person strikes you on the right cheek, turn and offer him the other.’” (Matt. 5:38,39)

 

 Turn the other cheek, as being kind is more important than being right.  Or as Yeshua puts it in the Gospel of Thomas, “Be passersby.” (GTh, 42)  Get not wrapped up in conflict, but rather live in peace.  In Luke,

“Love your enemies, do good to those who hate you; bless those who curse you and pray for those who maltreat you.  When someone slaps you on one cheek, turn and give him the other.”
(Luke 6:27-29)

 

Yeshua extends this premise to non-human’s as well in denouncing animal sacrifice.  In Matthew he states, “It is mercy I desire and not sacrifice.”  No violence, whatsoever – whether in obtainment of food, sacrifice for religious rites or in retaliation for a wrongdoing.  Also in Matthew, Yeshua commands, “love your enemies, pray for your persecutors.  This will prove that you are sons of your heavenly father.”(Matt. 5:44,45) nJohn, as usual, articulates this concept quite more obscurely in 15:27, “’Peace’ is my farewell to you, my peace is my gift to you; I do not give it to you as the world gives peace.’”  Also, simply stated in Mark as, “’You shall love your neighbor as yourself.’”(12:31)  Indeed, the “golden rule” includes one’s enemies.  Not only does this thought appear in all four canonical texts and also in the Gospel of Thomas: an excellent indication that they were actually the words and teachings of Yeshua, himself; but they also are repeated in each under the concept of the “golden rule.”

 

Judge Not, or Embrace Diversity

Here we revisit the Golden Rule again and explore it even further.  Yeshua taught to both men and women; both genders followed him and loved him.  However, the “original twelve” are all men!  Is this the result of a patriarchal church or is it the truth?  The former is a more likely scenario since women disciples are mentioned in non-canonical texts and some of the early priests were women as well.  The Gnostics held no sexual hierarchy or discrimination.  In the Gospel of Thomas, Peter speaks to Yeshua and pleads for Mary (Magdalen) to leave them.

“Simon Peter said to them, ‘Mary should leave us, for females are not worthy of life.’ Jesus said, ‘See, I am going to attract her to make her male so that she too might become a living spirit that resembles you males. For every female (element)[24] that makes itself male will enter the kingdom of heaven.” (GTh, 114)

 

By Peter asking that Mary leave them, insinuates that Mary was, indeed, a disciple.  That she was not worthy to be one of them.  Misogyny and patriarchal notions ran rampant in Yeshua’s time, but it was not always so.  Around the time of Yeshua’s birth, Yahweh (the Judaic God) still had wives.  The balance of the sexes remained.  Some of Yeshua’s disciples, like Simon Peter, were ostensibly threatened by women – especially Mary.  Peter couldn’t understand in his alleged misogynistic upbringing how his savior could love a woman more than himself.  For the Gospel of Phillip states,

“And the companion of the [Christ was] Mary Magdalen. [He] loved her more than [all] the disciples, [and he used to] kiss her on her [mouth, more] often than the rest of the [disciples.] They said to him, ‘Why do you love her more than all of us?’  The savior answered, saying to them, ‘Why do I not love you like her?  If a blind person and one with sight are both in the darkness, they are not different from one another.  When the light comes, then the person with sight will see the light, and the blind person will remain in the darkness.” (GPh, 48)

 

Phillip also states how three women “always used to walk with the lord – Mary his mother, his sister, and the Magdalene, who is called his companion.”  Notice how the author of Phillip calls her “the Magdalene.”  This supports the previously stated notion that Magdalen was not a surname, but rather an epithet meaning “great, exalted one.”  Phillip continues, “For ‘Mary’ is the name of his sister and his mother, and it is the name of his partner.” (GPh, 28)  Yeshua did not discriminate between the sexes just as he did not discriminate between classes.  He accepted and loved the tax collector as well as the leper; the man as well as the woman;  the human as well as the non-human.

Adhering to the notion of the celebration of diversity and the abstinence from discrimination, so it follows to withhold judgment.  Yeshua tells us in Matt. 7:1 “If you want to avoid judgment, stop passing judgment,” and in Luke 7:36-38 “Be compassionate, as your Father is compassionate.  Do not judge, and you will not be judged.  Do not condemn, and you will not be condemned.”  In John, this concept lies hidden among the verse, so I cannot easily quote it.  Throughout the “Role of Judea” and the “Last Discourse” lingers the theme of non-judgment and unconditional love. (John 13:18-35, 14:22-31, 15:9-27)  Just from living day to day, one realizes the danger in passing judgment – even on oneself.  This concept appears only in three of the four canonical texts; however, it certainly fits with the overall teachings of Yeshua.  I choose to believe this concept is, indeed, a valid teaching of Yeshua.

 

The Kingdom of Heaven is Within You.

Through the Gnostic Gospels, a taste of Yeshua’s original teaching remain on the subject of true salvation.  Just as Buddhism teaches meditation and self-exploration and the Greek Philosophers encouraged their followers to “Know Thyself,” so Yeshua also taught his disciples to look within to find the Kingdom of Heaven.  In the Gnostic Gospel of Thomas, Yeshua said,

“When you become acquainted with yourselves, then you will be recognized.  And you will understand that it is you who are children of the living father.  But if you do not become acquainted with yourselves, then you are in poverty, and it is you who are the poverty.” (GTh, 3)

 

Yeshua teaches his disciples that not only he is the son of The Divine, but we all are his children.  Yeshua saw it all more clearly than the rest of us; however, he is no more the sole son of God[25] than I am the sole daughter of God.  We all are the children of The Divine.  Yeshua says in Matthew, “You are the light of the world,” speaking to all his disciples. (Matt. 5:14)

He also teaches that the Kingdom of Heaven does not exist out there or up there somewhere, but rather within each of us, within everything.  Also in the Gospel of Thomas, Yeshua said,

“It is I who am the light (that presides) over all.  It is I who am the entirety:  it is from me that the entirety has come, and to me that the entirety goes.  Split a piece of wood:  I am there.  Lift a stone, and you will find me there.” (GTh 77)

 

Yeshua tells his disciples that even in stones and trees he is there; we all are.  For we all are one – it is all the same.  The Divine runs through Yeshua, but s/he also runs through you and me, the animals, the trees, and even the rocks.  “Blessed is that which existed before coming into being.  If you exist as my disciples and listen to my saying, these stones will minister unto you.” (GTh, 19)

Unfortunately, after the crucifixion and death of Yeshua in c. 26-36 C.E., his words and teaching were twisted beyond recognition.  And, even more unfortunately, scores of text were burned after the conversion of Constantine at the command of the “New Catholic Church” around the Council of Nicene in 325 C.E.  Here many canonical scriptures and a few non-canonical scriptures have been cited.  Many of his teachings will never be proven historically – only through the oral tradition of those original followers that were not suppress or martyred and what we truly know in our hearts and souls remain of this great teacher.  We can only compare the frequency in which a philosophy or story appears in canonical and non-canonical texts.  For his own brother and disciples were claimed heretics and excommunicated by the new church, and the words of Paul shone forth.  The rest is left up to our own souls to define; for that, we must truly know ourselves.

Chapter VI

How to be a True Christian, i.e.  Christ-like

 

“The purer I try to become, the nearer I feel to be to God.”  ~ Gandhi

 

  1. Be Vegetarian

The single most important thing you can do to begin your new life as a True Christian is to become a vegetarian.  Whether or not you believe in the theory that Yeshua was vegetarian, although historical evidence points in that direction, is irrelevant.  Yeshua shows us his respect for non-human life in Matthew 6:26, “Look at the birds in the sky.  They do not sow or reap, they gather nothing into barns;  yet the heavenly Father feeds them… Learn a lesson from the way the wild flowers grow.”

The capacity to kill for food is a natural drive in carnivores, but not so in herbivores.  Human beings are herbivores by nature.  We even see this illustrated in Genesis when God gave Adam and Eve “every seed-bearing plant all over the earth and every tree that has seed-bearing fruit on it to be your food.” (Gen. 1:29)  Once this drive is activated in herbivores, the drive to kill is not satiated with meat, as it is in natural carnivores, rather it remains active.  Then the carnivorous herbivore enjoys killing for killing’s sake.  They lack the limiting factor. (Vaclavik, 8)  No repugnance for killing, wars or violence remain in the carnivorous herbivore.  Once out of the meat-eating culture, one once again sees the repugnance of not only killing, wars and violence, but also repugnance at the idea of consuming rotting flesh.  There is nothing at all natural about the way humans consume animal flesh.  The natural carnivore kills and then plunges into the bowels of its prey, raw and bloody.  Humans, however, do everything possible to cover up the natural taste of dead meat.  They season, marinate and cook the flesh to make it palatable.  Due to the continued consumption of meat, those carnivorous herbivores fail to see the connection between eating meat and violence.  This lust for killing and violence manifests itself in many ways throughout our society, today:  bar brawls, hunting, football, hockey, other such contact sports, road rage, etc.  Sometimes this violence manifests itself vicariously through murder mysteries, action films and violent cartoons.

Regardless as to whether or not you believe Yeshua was actually a vegetarian two millennia ago, he most definitely would be vegetarian if he were alive today in this society,.  With 16 million animals slaughtered for food every day in this country alone, how could he not be?  This also doesn’t mention the horrendous lives these sentient animals lead up to the point of slaughter.  For any person who truly respects life, the earth and has any sense of compassion, vegetarianism is the only logical and compassionate diet of our time:  for your own health and wellness, the health and wellness of our planet and the health and wellness of our animal siblings.  This remains a book on discovering the true essence of Christianity, not to preach vegetarianism and the cruelties of factory farming; but you may find out more about the conditions of factory farming at www.factoryfarming.org among others found in the Bibliography and Resources section at the back of this book.  Remember, just because you yourself do not hold the tool by which these animals die, you hold the resources that continue to make it possible for so many millions to be murdered and abused each day.  That resource is in your wallet, and you make life changing decisions for countless, faceless millions each day:  human and non-human, alike.

 

  1. Do unto others as you would have them do unto you.

The golden rule, written in Matthew as “Treat others the way you would have them treat you.” We have all heard it, but how many of us actually live it?  This rule holds true with all the things you don’t even know you are doing!  Be conscious about your choices in consumption.  Avoid purchasing things made in sweat-shops, not an easy task these days.  Buy locally when you can.  Support Organic Farmers.  Buy second hand or used.  Try to be aware, but not inundated, with what is going on politically and socially.  On a more personal and individual level, make a daily agreement with yourself not to anger or judge just for today.  To accept everything your receive, material or otherwise, as a beautiful gift.  You are of The Divine just as your neighbor, partner, child, dog, cat, etc.

Do not support zoos or aquariums unless you, too, would like to be taken away from your family and jailed.  Do not support sweat shops unless you, too, would like to work in deplorable conditions for pennies an hour.  Do not be rude or impatient.  Do not be condescending or judgmental. 

     Be kind.  Emulate peace and serenity with your every thought, word and action.  Be understanding and accepting of those who are different than you, because ultimately, it is all the same.  Show respect for every living thing.

Be impeccable with your word.  This abolishes the need for oaths and promises.  Pythagoras taught this, and so did Yeshua.  In Matthew, Yeshua says, “Say, ‘Yes when you mean ‘Yes’ and ‘No’ when you mean ‘No.’  Anything beyond that is from the evil one.” (Matt. 5:37)  Promises and oaths, however, are cultural in present times; so remain true to those oaths and promises.  Be true to your word, whether given as a promise or not.

I cannot list everything here; however, I invite each of you to become mindful of your actions.  Look within to find the answers, they are there.  You only need ask the Divine essence that lives within each of you.  “Ask, and you will receive.  Seek and you will find.  Knock, and it will be opened to you.” (Matt. 7:7)  The kingdom of heaven lies within.

 

  1. Answer violence with peace.

     I have seen many bumper stickers and such that read WWJD – meaning ‘What Would Jesus Do?’  What a wonderful thing to keep in mind!  After these horrible and tragic terrorist attacks on US soil – What Would Jesus Do?  Would he “bomb them into the stone age?”  Would he “nuke the bastards?”[26]  I have a stinking suspicion that Jesus, that is Yeshua, would follow his own teaching to “love your enemies” and “turn the other cheek.”  He would be opposed to the bombing even if the entire Taliban were guilty of the American attacks.  Two wrongs do not make a right, as the cliché goes.  If we are to be truly Christians, we should strive to emulate Christ’s actions and teachings.

On a more personal level, emulate Christ’s principals at work, at school, in social situations and driving on the road.  If cut off in traffic, exude compassion and understanding instead of screaming out the window, laying on your horn and giving them the finger.  If your boss scolds or chastises, acknowledge his/her frustration and know it remains his/her issue.  Do not take it on yourself.  Graciously accept the hand dealt.  Recognize all as a gift, even if it appears to be negative – perhaps it is an opportunity for self-examination and reflection:  a priceless gift, indeed!  Which segues into:

 

  1. Look within to find the kingdom of heaven

Meditate, journey, walk in silent contemplation.  Find out who you really are, not who your parents, your school, your spouse, your friends or your church told you.  Be conscious with every word, action and deed.  Realize that when someone is cruel to you, it is not about you but rather about them and their issues.  You can be a more pure example of a true Christian to shine forth and help that person who acts cruelly.  Just by example – it is a far more powerful tool than any sermon or argument.  Most importantly, find out who you truly are – and then be that person.  For Yeshua says in Matthew, “In the same way, your light must shine before men so that they may see goodness in your acts and give praise to your heavenly father.” (Matt. 5:16) 

A plethora of information is available on different paths to the divine.  See what fits the best.  Explore yourself and your spirituality.  Get to know yourself and the Divine that lives within.

 

 

  1. Judge not, lest you be judged, or embrace diversity

Certainly, judge not others, but this is the easy part.  Be gentle with yourself!  Stop yourself from calling yourself “stupid” or “idiot.”  Love yourself, first.  Put it into practice in every thought, action and deed.  Those around you will be happier if you, yourself, are happy first.  If you spend life trying to please others, you miss the point every time because, ultimately, those around you will not be happy if you continue to deprive yourself and remain miserable.  Affirm your own Divinity with each thought, word and action.

 

If you need a more recent example of truly Christian behavior, look to Gandhi. He emulated everything Yeshua stood for and taught.  He freed India from British Rule through peaceful actions only.  Even if you just rent the movie, look into the life of this amazing man.  One important thing to realize, although this man was Christian – he was not Christian.

Most importantly – try.  Just because you can’t do everything doesn’t mean you shouldn’t do anything.  Perhaps, like the majority of our society, you are addicted to meat.  Please do not throw this all away saying ‘I can’t do it!’  Rather take small steps.  Try to give up read meat first and switch to all organically raised chicken.  Then, after about a month or two, cut down your poultry and fish intake to every other day instead of every day.  Ask a vegetarian friend for help choosing substitutes, or visit one of the many websites I’ve listed in the Additional Resources section below.  Many of them offer free kits to help make the transition go more smoothly.

Let us say you have a problem with anger and being very judgmental, another common distraction in our society.  Don’t just throw your hands up and say I am who I am – try to be more Christian by not judging yourself or being hard on yourself for making a mistake, getting angry, or being judgmental.  Try forgiving yourself and then trying again.  Acknowledge your mistake or anger, realize it, and then let it go.  Then try, try again.  It is all about practice and intent and looking within.

I conclude this work with a quote from Yeshua written in Matthew:

“Anyone who hears my words and puts them into practice is like the wise man who built his house on the rock.  When the rainy season set in, the torrents cam and the winds blew and buffeted his house.  It did not collapse; it had been solidly set on rock.  Anyone who hears my words but does not put them into practice is like the foolish man who built his house on sandy ground.  The rains fell, the torrents came, this winds blew and lashed against his house.  It collapsed under all this and was completely ruined.” (Matt. 7:24-27)

 

Together, we have now discovered his words behind the dogma – be like the wise man, and put them into practice.

Namaste.
